i.
Acoustic separation between infusion bays
Zone · Infusion Bay
Confidence
4/5
Recommended parameters
Bay-to-bay spacing≥ 8 ft c-c
Partition height66 – 72 in
Ceiling NRC≥ 0.85
Ambient noise (day)≤ 45 dBA
STC partition rating35 – 40
Design rationale
"I could hear the alarms from other bays — it made it impossible to nap." Forty-two such comments locate the acoustic field as the dominant source of distress in the bay. The parameters above are the minimum thresholds at which patient-reported alarm intrusion drops below a measurable threshold in the cited studies.
FGI 2022 §3.7-2.2.4 Jalalianhosseini et al. (2025) HERD 18(1), 76–94

ii.
Visual privacy at the infusion station
Zone · Infusion Bay
Confidence
4/5
Recommended parameters
Curtain extensionfloor-to-ceiling
Gap closure≤ 2 in
Privacy materialOpaque, antimicrobial
Patient sightlinesOutward · windowed
Design rationale
Thirty-six records describe loss of privacy at the most vulnerable moments — receiving difficult news, weeping, vomiting. The current curtain detail fails not because it is the wrong material, but because its dimensional shortfall (6-inch gap at the floor) violates the patient's basic spatial dignity.
Planetree Standards (2021) FGI 2022 §2.5 Cai & Zimring (2018)

How can I improve infusion bay privacy without losing nurse line-of-sight?
心
The constraint you describe is real — partition height that ensures patient privacy at the head and lap (the most vulnerable sightlines) does not need to extend to ceiling. A 66–72 in tall partition with a translucent 12 in upper light band preserves both: patient privacy at eye level, and nurse station scan-ability.
Partition height (opaque)66 – 72 in
Translucent band height12 in (84 in total)
Bay-to-bay c-c spacing≥ 8 ft
Nurse station throw distance≤ 32 ft to bay center
